Henri Badaröh is a Brazilian visual artist and writer.

Through queer, decolonial and intersectional approaches, he focuses on the dialogues between photography and film, analogue creative practices and new media, image and written word, Europe and [America](https://youtu.be/Q2iGiYHo1Eg).

Based in the Netherlands, Henri has been active in the arts and culture field for many years, collaborating multidisciplinarily with artists on publications and performances worldwide. He has also conducted portfolio reviews and mentoring sessions for World Press Photo's Joop Swart Masterclass, FUTURES Photography, Foam Amsterdam, Fomu Antwerp, and the Royal Academy of Art, The Hague (KABK), amongst others. Between 2019 and 2024, Henri co-edited Foam Magazine, collaborating with prominent artists like Rosana Paulino, Alfredo Jaar, Dana Lixenberg, Sunil Gupta, Miranda July, Ocean Vuong, and Frida Orupabo.

He holds an MA in Film and Photographic Studies from Leiden University (2019) and a BA in Photography from Senac São Paulo (2015).
